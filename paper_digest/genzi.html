It works because GenZI leverages the rich semantic understanding embedded in large vision-language models to bridge the gap between 2D generative capabilities and 3D geometric reasoning. Conclusionï¼ˆç»“è®ºï¼‰

This paper presents GenZI, the first zero-shot approach for generating 3D human-scene interactions without requiring any 3D interaction training data. By distilling interaction priors from large vision-language models, GenZI leverages dynamic masked inpainting to create plausible 2D human-scene compositions and robust 3D lifting optimization to synthesize physically consistent 3D interactions. The framework demonstrates high flexibility and generality across diverse indoor and outdoor scenes, controlled through simple text prompts and point locations. Extensive experiments and user studies show that GenZI produces more natural and physically plausible interactions compared to existing methods, while being applicable to arbitrary 3D environments without the constraints of supervised learning approaches. This work establishes a new paradigm for data-efficient 3D human-scene interaction synthesis that leverages advances in 2D generative models.
