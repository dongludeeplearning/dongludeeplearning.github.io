Conclusionï¼ˆç»“è®ºï¼‰

This paper introduces Motion-R1, a novel framework that combines decomposed Chain-of-Thought reasoning with reinforcement learning binding to enhance text-to-motion generation. By addressing the dual challenges of capturing temporal/causal complexities in natural language and simplifying RL-based approaches, Motion-R1 achieves state-of-the-art performance across multiple benchmark datasets. The Decomposed CoT Data Engine enables structured reasoning about complex motion sequences, while RL Binding provides efficient multi-modal alignment without costly human annotations. Extensive experiments demonstrate superior results in motion quality, semantic fidelity, and diversity, establishing Motion-R1 as a scalable and interpretable solution for high-quality motion generation from natural language descriptions.
