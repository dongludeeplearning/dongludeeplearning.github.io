Conclusionï¼ˆç»“è®ºï¼‰

This paper presents GaussianAvatars, a novel method for creating photorealistic head avatars using 3D Gaussian splats rigged to parametric morphable face models. The approach enables full control over expression, pose, and viewpoint while achieving superior rendering quality and animation fidelity. By establishing explicit geometric correspondences between Gaussians and mesh triangles, and implementing a binding inheritance strategy for adaptive density control, the method overcomes the limitations of traditional radiance field approaches. Extensive experiments demonstrate significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods in both novel-view synthesis and reenactment tasks, establishing GaussianAvatars as a new standard for controllable photorealistic avatar creation.
