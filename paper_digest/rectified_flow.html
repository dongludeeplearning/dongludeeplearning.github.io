Conclusionï¼ˆç»“è®ºï¼‰

This paper introduces rectified flow, a surprisingly simple yet powerful approach to learning neural ODE models for transporting between distributions. By learning to follow straight paths connecting paired points, rectified flow provides a unified solution to generative modeling and domain transfer that avoids the instability of GANs, the intractability of MLE methods, and the high computational costs of existing ODE/SDE models. The reflow procedure produces increasingly straight flows that can be simulated with single Euler steps while maintaining theoretical guarantees of reduced transport costs. Empirical results demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 (FID 4.85, recall 0.51), image-to-image translation, and domain adaptation, establishing rectified flow as a foundational framework for efficient and reliable distribution transport.
