Conclusionï¼ˆç»“è®ºï¼‰

This paper introduces CTF, a coarse-to-fine token prediction framework that addresses the vocabulary complexity challenge in autoregressive image generation. By leveraging k-means clustering to group visually similar tokens and employing a two-stage prediction process, CTF maintains the benefits of large codebooks for high-quality reconstruction while significantly simplifying the autoregressive modeling task. Extensive experiments demonstrate superior performance on ImageNet, with up to 1-point FID reduction and 59-point Inception Score improvement, while achieving faster sampling speeds despite the additional inference step. The framework's modular design enables seamless integration with various autoregressive architectures, establishing a new paradigm for efficient and high-quality image generation.
