Conclusionï¼ˆç»“è®ºï¼‰

This paper introduces TalkingGaussian, a deformation-based framework for high-fidelity 3D talking head synthesis using Gaussian Splatting. By maintaining a persistent head structure and representing facial motions through smooth deformations rather than difficult appearance changes, the method avoids facial distortions common in NeRF-based approaches. The face-mouth decomposition strategy addresses motion inconsistencies between facial regions, enabling accurate reconstruction of both coarse expressions and fine mouth articulations. Extensive experiments demonstrate superior performance in image quality, lip synchronization, and rendering speed compared to state-of-the-art methods, achieving 108 FPS rendering with high visual fidelity.
