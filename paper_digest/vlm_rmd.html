Conclusionï¼ˆç»“è®ºï¼‰

This work presents a groundbreaking approach to HOI synthesis that leverages VLMs for automatic reward design and goal state construction. The VLM-Guided Relative Movement Dynamics framework enables the first unified physics-based HOI system capable of long-horizon interactions with diverse object types. By introducing the Interplay dataset and demonstrating superior performance across both single-task and multi-task scenarios, the work establishes a new paradigm for HOI research that eliminates manual reward engineering while maintaining physical plausibility and semantic fidelity. The framework's ability to handle open-set objects and complex interaction sequences paves the way for more natural and versatile human-object interaction synthesis in applications ranging from robotics to virtual reality.
