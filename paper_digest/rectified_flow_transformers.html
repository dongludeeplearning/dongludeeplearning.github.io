Conclusionï¼ˆç»“è®ºï¼‰

This paper establishes rectified flow as the superior generative modeling approach for high-resolution text-to-image synthesis by introducing improved noise sampling techniques that bias training towards perceptually relevant scales. The novel multimodal DiT architecture with separate weights for image and text modalities enables bidirectional information flow, significantly improving text comprehension, typography, and human preference ratings. Through comprehensive scaling studies, the authors demonstrate predictable trends where lower validation loss strongly correlates with improved performance across various metrics and human evaluations. The largest models, reaching up to 8B parameters, outperform state-of-the-art open-source models like SDXL and Pixart-Î±, as well as closed-source models like DALL-E 3, in prompt understanding and overall image quality. This work provides the foundation for establishing rectified flow transformers as the new standard practice in generative image synthesis.
