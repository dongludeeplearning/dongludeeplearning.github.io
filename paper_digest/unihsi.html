It works because UniHSI addresses the core challenge of interaction diversity by decomposing complex behaviors into fundamental contact primitives. The CoC representation captures the essential physics of human-object interactions while remaining abstract enough to generalize across scenarios. By leveraging LLMs for high-level planning and physics simulation for low-level execution, the framework creates a powerful synergy between symbolic reasoning and physical constraints, enabling truly versatile and controllable human-scene interactions.

Conclusionï¼ˆç»“è®ºï¼‰

This work presents UniHSI, the first unified framework for language-guided human-scene interaction that supports versatile control through natural language commands. By formulating interactions as Chain of Contacts and leveraging LLMs for high-level planning with physics-based execution, UniHSI achieves remarkable performance across diverse interaction types and scenarios. The framework's key innovation lies in its ability to unify previously disparate approaches into a cohesive system that eliminates the need for task-specific engineering and manual annotations. Through comprehensive experiments on the newly introduced ScenePlan dataset, we demonstrate superior performance in both controlled synthetic environments and real scanned scenes. The results establish UniHSI as a significant advancement toward practical human-scene interaction systems, opening new possibilities for applications in embodied AI, virtual reality, and human-computer interaction. The unified approach not only improves performance but also significantly reduces the engineering complexity required for implementing diverse interaction capabilities.
